The helicopter shot, popularized by cricketer MS Dhoni, involves a 360-degree bat rotation1. To play this shot, the stance and footwork are crucial. Dhoni's stance is slightly open, with feet and shoulders width apart. As the bowler approaches, he takes his front foot forward while keeping the back foot anchored, allowing him to generate power and momentum for the shot2.
